And I find happiness in




Reading



Travelling





Exploring New Tastes


Reading creates an alternate reality for me.It is a direct mode of conversation between the author and the reader. It has made me realise that the way of perceiving defines who we actually are. "Merchant of Venice" by William Shakespeare is my all-time favourite. Whenever life around me goes rough, there will always be a character to relate with. And the biggest perk of reading, as I understand it, is that you get to easily camouflage with the world around you, for reading broadens our mind.

There is nothing quite like traveling, like seeing a new place for the first time or returning to a favorite one.It is merely a superior feeling: to go to another country, and to see people live differently, speak differently, look differently. This is how we come to understand how big and crazy our world is.Something I realised from travelling is that you get to know what "being home" actually means. This forces us to be adventurous, to live life to the fullest, to make the most of this precious gift and use the time we have to discover new things, and meet new people. There are no hesitations.
Go and Explore.

The best part of every travel is the different tastes you discover. I have seen people who eat like, literally, nothing in the name of diet. But I, on the other hand, am a foodie. I believe that what we eat becomes good or bad based on our intentions. If we honor the cow when we eat cheese, it will be good for our body. When we go to another place, what they serve us is a token of their love and respect to us.

But life ain't all about doing the deeds that bring us happiness. Every chapter of life is a realisation of who we are and what we are capable of acheiving and accomplishing. We acheive skills over time, be it academic or non-academic, just like I became good at public speaking.
